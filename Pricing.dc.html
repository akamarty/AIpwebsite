FAQ

Questions, answered

Where does the option-code data come from?

Every explanation is generated against factory build-sheet data and cross-checked option by option — not a generic acronym list. Coverage spans every 911, 718, and classic chassis code we track.

Why not just read the factory build sheet myself?

You can — if you already know what X51 or PTS means. Sophia reads the codes and explains, in plain English, what each one actually did to the car and why it matters to a buyer or collector, in seconds instead of a forum search.

Is the free plan actually useful?

Yes — unlimited VIN decodes, every factory option explained, and the full spec sheet, free forever. Pro and Collector unlock the rest of the buying report: fair market value, collector desirability, comparable sales, ownership costs, common issues, and negotiation strategy.

Can I cancel anytime?

Anytime, in one click from your account. You keep access through the end of your billing period.

What if my VIN's model or chassis isn't supported yet?

The VIN structure decode covers 1981–present chassis; the option-code reference covers 1964–2003. Collector members get priority support if your exact car isn't fully covered yet.

Where does the Spec Rarity ranking come from?

Real production and factory color-distribution data, not a guess — see the free Market Data page for the 718 lineup we've loaded so far (production counts by trim, exterior color breakdowns for GT4 and Spyder, Spyder RS allocation). Pro/Collector tiers apply this same data to your exact VIN's spec.
